\paragraph{The problem of plant diseases}
Our lives would not be possible without agriculture. We use goods like medicine, pesticides, and rubber everyday, all of which are made from plants. Plants are ubiquitous in our lives, and they fulfill some of our essential needs. Most importantly, plants feed most people globally \cite{mccouchFeedingFuture2013}. But many of us still suffer from hunger. In 2024, it was estimated that between 638 and 720 million people did not have enough to eat \cite{StateFoodSecurity2025}. Plant dieases make this situation even worse by robbing us of food \cite{carvajal-yepesGlobalSurveillanceSystem2019, heHighThroughputRoboticPhenotyping2025, nazarovInfectiousPlantDiseases2020, hughesOpenAccessRepository2016}. Therefore, they are a direct threat to our livlihoods.
\subparagraph{}
Plant diseases make it difficult for crops to survive and stay healthy \cite{agriosINTRODUCTION2005}. They interfere with a plant's cells causing them to malfunction or die \cite{huberRelationshipNutritionPlant2012}. And each cell helps the plant carry out essential life-sustaining functions: conducting photosynthesis, and absorbing water and nutrients. So, when these cells are damaged, the plant's ability to obtain and use nutrients is limited. As a result, plant diseases stunt the growth, and decrease the yield and nutritional value of crops \cite{savaryCropLossesDue2012, vyskaTradeoffDiseaseResistance2016}. In some cases, the damage is so severe that farmers lose up to 100\% of their crops \cite{nazarovInfectiousPlantDiseases2020, dongPlantPADPlatformLargescale2024}. However, the losses caused by plant diseases goes further than just the yield and quality of crops; they extend to rural communities, consumers, the environment, and governments \cite{savaryCropLossesDue2012}. Therefore, plant diseases threaten the health of crops, and this has disastrous far-reaching effects on our livlihoods. Clearly, we need solutions to deal with them.

\paragraph{Some solutions to plant disease}
Luckily, several options exist. For example, plant breeding is an environmentally sustainable and cost-effective solution, which helps to produce disease-resistant varieties of plants \cite{anandBreedingTechniquesDispense2023, ortonIntroduction2020, heHighThroughputRoboticPhenotyping2025, furbankPhenomicsTechnologiesRelieve2011}. Alternativly, we also resort to precision agriculture, which uses data from crops and their environment to make optimal decesions about agricultural actions \cite{PrecisionAgricultureFood2025, PlantDiseaseDetection, cisternasSystematicLiteratureReview2020}. For instance, precision agriculture aids farmers in deciding on the best way to apply pesticides during a pathogen epidemic \cite{bockPlantDiseaseSeverity2010} by helping determine when to apply pesticides, how much pesticide to apply, and where to apply it; as a result, such precise pesticide application will reduce costs, save resources, and benefit the environment and human health \cite{pathakCurrentStatusPesticide2022, reiser3DImagingSonar2017, khalidRealTimePlantHealth2023}. So, plant breeding and precision agriculture help deal with plant diseases; however, these methods depend on effective plant phenotyping \cite{fahlgrenVersatilePhenotypingSystem2015, furbankPhenomicsTechnologiesRelieve2011a, gehanPlantCVV2Image2017, zhangBOCNNBiLSTMDeepLearning2024, zhuDeepLearningbasedText2025a, anandBreedingTechniquesDispense2023}.

\paragraph{The relationship between plant diseases and plant phenotyping}
Plant phenotyping is the evaulation of observable or measurable plant phenotypes \cite{fioraniFutureScenariosPlant2013, johannsenGenotypeConceptionHeredity1911a}, which are proxy measurements or traits indicating a plant's stress, health or growth in relation to its environment \cite{fioraniFutureScenariosPlant2013, liReviewImagingTechniques2014}. Moreover, phenotypes represent traits of an individual plant in terms of all of its chemichal, physilogical, morphological, and behavioral aspects \cite{guoPhenotypingPlants2014, mohammedReviewPlantPhenotyping2025, liReviewImagingTechniques2014}. So, plant phenotyping provides quantative data that represents various aspects of a plant. When a plant is diseased its form, function and integrity are affected \cite{agriosINTRODUCTION2005}, therefore reliable measurments of all aspects of plant are required in plant disease solutions. Consequently, plant phenotyping is an effective quantative method for plant breeding and precision agriculture \cite{gehanPlantCVV2Image2017, wenThermalImagingDigital2023, dongPlantPADPlatformLargescale2024, mutkaImagebasedPhenotypingPlant2015a}.
