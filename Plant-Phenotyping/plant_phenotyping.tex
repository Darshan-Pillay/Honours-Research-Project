\paragraph{Context for plant phenotyping?}
It is of vital importance in plant breeding to be able to propogate desirable heritable traits from plants through controlled human action \cite{ortonIntroduction2020}. This is especially important in the context of food security where a cultivars resistant to adverse abiotic and biotic stresses affect the final overall crop yield. The literature indicates that plant phenotyping has become an effective tool in plant breeding methods. \cite{anandBreedingTechniquesDispense2023}.

\paragraph{Points about plant phneotyping}
 Plant phenotyping is then the evaulation of phenotypes in plants with the main goal of producing quantative data to study the dynamic interaction between a plant and its environment  \cite{fioraniFutureScenariosPlant2013}.

\paragraph{Explanation of relevance of plant phenotyping for current study?}
The term phenotype was first employed by Johannsen \cite{johannsenGenotypeConceptionHeredity1911a}. Who stated that a phenotype is all types of organism which are idenfitied from one antoher by inspection or by some measuremment. So it is an obserable primitive trait or a complex trait of a plant that can be identified visually or measured and evaulated numerically. Therfore the data produced by plant phenotyping assist in choosing the genetic material (germplasm) that will enable plant breeders to induce traits in cultivars which are optimal for various diffrent growing environments (like water scarce environments).

 \paragraph{Why plant phenotyping in the context of this study?}
 Conventional plant plantphenotyping suffers from scalabality issues and is often constrained by costs, time, and human effort required. Furthermore many methods are destructive to the cultivar, making it hard to replicate, and compare measurements of phenotypes over time through the ontogensis of a cultivar. However in the past decade image processing techniques have been applied successfully to aquire phenotypical data in a non-destructive matter \cite{arnalbarbedoDigitalImageProcessing2013}.. There exists many different image techniques that have been applied in greenhouses and in the field which allow for the assesment of resistance to disease, biomass, soli salinity, etc using various differenr sensors, and techniques.
