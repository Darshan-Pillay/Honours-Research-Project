In food security, and plant breeding it is desirable to identify cultivars which have desirable resitsnaces against abiotic and biotic stresses. For example, given two cultivars A and B in a water scarce environment we would like to identify the which plant fairs the best. Best in the sense of biomass, resistant to diesease, metablic health. All of these observable properties of the cultivars that can be quantified and measure enable us to make decisions to choose and breed cultivars with genes most suited for specific growing environemnts whilst minimzing loss and maximising yield. This process or field of study which provides the mechansim for this is called Plant Phenotyping.

\paragraph{What is plant phenotyping?}
It is of vital importance in agriculture to identify the best cultivars for certain environments, especially in the context of food security where adverse abiotic and biotic stresses affect crop yield.
